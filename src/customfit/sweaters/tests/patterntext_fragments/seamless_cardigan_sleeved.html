Knitting Your Sweater Mostly Seamlessly

If you’d like to make Seamless Test Design in a mostly seamless way, here’s how:

Cast on the total number of body stitches, front and back together:

CO 47 stitches, place side seam marker, CO 98, place side seam marker, CO 47. 192 stitches total.

Work in hem stitch and then main pattern stitch, including waist shaping for front and back, as written in instructions above (but on all body stitches at once).

When you reach armhole shaping, work back and forth on front and back stitches separately as written in the pattern.

When body pieces are complete, seam shoulders using mattress stitch.

Knit sleeves from the top down in the round as follows:

With RS facing and beginning at top shoulder seam, pick up and knit 67 stitches around armhole edge. You will now shape the sleeve caps using short rows.

Set-up Row 1 (RS): Knit 10 stitches, wrap the next stitch, turn your work.

Set-up Row 2 (WS): Purl 21 stitches, wrap the next stitch, turn your work.

Continue working short rows as established, working one additional stitch each row and working wraps with wrapped stitches, until 16 stitches remain unworked at armhole, ending with a WS row.

Next row (RS): Knit across all stitches, joining for working in the round. You have shaped the sleeve cap and have 67 stitches for sleeve.

Work down the arm in the round, reversing shaping as described in the Sleeve section of pattern, until you reach the end of the sleeve. BO all stitches.

Work any edge trim stitches as described in Finishing.
